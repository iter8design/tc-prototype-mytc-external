Official languages notice

Transport Canada respects the Official Languages Act and the relevant Treasury Board policies, and is committed to ensuring all information and services on this site are available in both English and French. However, visitors should be aware that some information from external sources that are not subject to the Official Languages Act is only provided as a convenience and is available only in the language in which it was provided.

Information and services that are provided on this site in language(s) other than English or French are only provided as a convenience to the visitor when available.

Ownership and usage of content provided on this site

Materials on this Web site were produced and/or compiled by Transport Canada for the purpose of providing Canadians with direct access to information about the programs and services offered by the Government of Canada.

The material on this site is covered by the provisions of the Copyright Act, by Canadian laws, policies, regulations and international agreements. Such provisions serve to identify the information source and, in specific instances, to prohibit reproduction of materials without written permission.

Canada’s Copyright Act protects authors/creators who write something, take a photograph, design computer software, produce audiovisual materials, compose music, design maps, or draw plans or illustrations in either paper format or other mediums.

Crown copyright protects works created under the direction and control of Transport Canada.

Non-commercial reproduction

Information on this site, other than government symbols, has been posted with the intent that it be readily available for personal and public non-commercial use and may be reproduced, in part or in whole and by any means, without charge or further permission from Transport Canada, provided you do the following:

  • exercise due diligence in ensuring the accuracy of the materials reproduced;
  • name both the complete title of the original work, and Transport Canada as the source department; and
  • indicate that the reproduction is a copy of an official Transport Canada work and was not produced in affiliation with, or with the endorsement of Transport Canada.

Commercial reproduction

Reproduction of multiple copies of materials on this site, in whole or in part, for the purposes of commercial redistribution is prohibited except with written permission from Transport Canada.

Do I need permission (license) to reproduce Transport Canada works?

YES, if:

  • you revise, adapt or translate a Transport Canada work; or
  • you distribute your final product for commercial purposes.

In this case, complete the Application for Crown Copyright Clearance.

NO, if:

  • you reproduce a work (in part or in whole) for personal or public non-commercial purposes, or for cost-recovery purposes unless the material you wish to reproduce states you need permission.
    • A reproduction means making a copy of information as published, with no changes.
    • Personal and public non-commercial purposes mean distributing the reproduced information at no charge.
    • Cost-recovery means charging a fee to recover printing and other related costs.

In this case, refer to the Non-commercial Reproduction section for the terms of usage.
